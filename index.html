// 01: MACHINE LEARNING

The Problem with Basic Security

  • The Old Way: Platforms used to block accounts just for posting too fast or having no profile picture.
  • The New Threat: Scammers now use AI to act exactly like real humans. Basic filters don't work anymore.
  • Our Solution (ML): We train AI to look at how an account behaves, not just what it looks like.

// 02: MACHINE LEARNING

Spotting the Robotic Routine

Real Humans (Messy)

  • • We log in at random times.
  • • We scroll fast, then slow, and like random posts.
  • • We make typos when we chat.
  • AI sees this as normal, random behavior.

Fake Accounts (Perfect)

  • • They post on exact, scheduled timers.
  • • They only interact with specific target accounts.
  • • They copy-paste the exact same comments.
  • AI flags this as suspiciously "perfect".

// 03: MACHINE LEARNING

Face Matching AI

  • How it works: We use AI models that don't just "see" a face, they measure the exact distance between eyes, nose, and jaw.
  • The Search: It compares a suspect's profile picture against millions of real photos online in seconds.
  • The Catch: If an account is 2 weeks old, but has a 99% face match to a famous CEO... it's a confirmed clone.

// 04: MACHINE LEARNING

Catching Fake Typing Styles

  • Sudden Changes: If a boring, professional account suddenly starts using slang like "Buy Crypto Now!"—the AI knows the account got hacked.
  • Lazy ChatGPT Bots: We scan posts for obvious AI mistakes, like an account accidentally tweeting "As an AI language model..."

// 05: CYBERSECURITY DOMAIN

Finding Hidden Footprints

The Hidden ID Test

On X (Twitter), you can change your @name anytime. But your account has a permanent, hidden "ID Number" created the day you joined.

Proof: If the bio says "Joined 2018", but the hidden ID was created in 2026, they are lying.

Stolen Image Quality

When scammers download your photo and re-upload it to a fake account, the image loses quality. We can detect this invisible drop.

Proof: Missing camera data + bad image quality = Stolen media.

// 06: CYBERSECURITY DOMAIN

The "Mutual Friends" Check

  • Real Human Networks: In real life, your friends usually know each other. Your friend list looks like a densely connected web.
  • Fake Account Networks: Scammers randomly add random people to look popular. None of those people know each other.
  • The Verdict: If an account's friend group has zero mutual connections, it's almost certainly a scammer.

// 07: CYBERSECURITY DOMAIN

Legal Steps to Remove Fakes

  • Save the Proof: Before you report, screenshot the fake profile and copy their exact URL link. Sometimes they block you to hide.
  • Indian IT Act (Section 79): Platforms are legally required to remove impersonators if you provide proof.
  • Email Directly: The basic "Report" button uses slow AI. Email the platform's official "Grievance Officer" directly for fast human review.

// 08: LAYPERSON GUIDE

How Anyone Can Fight Back

1

Identify

Look closely at the username. Scammers use tricks like replacing a lowercase 'L' with a capital 'I'.

2

Isolate

Do NOT message the fake account. Immediately text your mutual friends and warn them not to accept the request.

3

Escalate

If they ask for money, report the profile immediately on cybercrime.gov.in.

// 09: NATIVE TAKEDOWN

Taking Down Instagram Fakes

Step 1: The Right Category

Never just click "Spam." Go to the profile > Three Dots (⋮) > Report > "Pretending to be someone else".

Step 2: The ID Selfie

If the scammer blocked you, you can't report from your app. Google "Instagram Impersonation Form" and submit a selfie holding your Aadhar card. A real human will review it and delete the fake.

Government ID verification bypasses the basic AI and forces a human employee to look at your case.

// 10: NATIVE TAKEDOWN

Taking Down Facebook Fakes

Facebook's security system reacts to numbers. More reports = Faster deletion.

Mass Reporting

Tell at least 5 friends to report the profile at the exact same time. Choose "Pretending to be a friend".

Report the Messages

If the fake is asking for money in Messenger, report the chat messages directly as a Scam. Financial scam reports get handled the fastest.

// 11: NATIVE TAKEDOWN

Taking Down X (Twitter) Fakes

Is it a Parody?

X allows joke accounts. To get them banned, you must prove they are pretending to be you to trick people, not just making a joke.

The Copyright Takedown

If the scammer uses your personal photo as their profile picture, report it as a Copyright Violation (DMCA) instead of impersonation. It works much faster.

Using copyright laws forces platforms to remove stolen images legally within hours.
